# Observability

Observability is the ability to understand the internal state of a system from its external outputs. The three pillars of observability are metrics, logs, and traces.

Metrics are numeric measurements aggregated over time, such as request rate and error rate. Logs are timestamped records of discrete events. Distributed traces follow a single request as it travels across multiple services.

A service level objective is a target value for a service level indicator measured over a window of time. An error budget is the amount of unreliability a service is allowed to accumulate before the team must stop shipping features and focus on reliability.
